Notification Service Architecture

Bounded context: Alerting · Stack: Node.js / Express · Code-level walkthrough: Notification Service

The notification-service realises the Alerting context. Architecturally it is a rule engine and dispatcher: it decides whether an event warrants an alert and fans it out to the right people across more than one channel.

Architectural Style

A layered Express service organised around two responsibilities that map directly onto the context’s two aggregates: managing where a principal can be reached, and what they have chosen to receive.

Internal Structure

Key Architectural Decisions

  • Two responsibilities, two models. Delivery channels and alert preferences are kept apart so a device can be revoked without losing choices, and choices can change without re-registering devices.
  • Dual-channel delivery. An alert is dispatched both as an in-app event (through the broker, to connected clients) and as a push to offline devices, so reachability does not depend on the app being open.
  • Repeat suppression. Because readings arrive continuously, the service is designed to debounce repeated alerts for the same condition rather than notify on every reading.
  • Recipient resolution by collaboration. The service asks the twin-service which domains a building belongs to and translates that into recipients, rather than keeping its own copy of building structure (an anti-corruption boundary).
  • Self-pruning channels. A delivery channel that the provider reports as permanently dead is removed automatically, keeping the model clean without a scheduled job.

Integration

  • Owns notification-db; no other service reads it.
  • Receives temperature events from sensor-service; resolves domains from twin-service; publishes alert events to the broker — see Communication & Data Flow.

For the throttle mechanics, VAPID delivery, the cleanup behaviour, and the API, see the Notification Service internals page.
